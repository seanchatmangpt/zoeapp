**A Four-Year Transformation of Operational Software into Avatar-Projected Object-Centric Process Fields**

*Independent Researcher*
*Truex / VKG Runtime Research Initiative*

## Abstract
This dissertation presents the design, implementation, and theoretical formalization of a new operational computing paradigm: the Virtual Knowledge Graph Hook Runtime (VKG-HR). Over a four-year research and implementation period, we transformed conventional workflow systems, CRUD applications, process automations, and role-based enterprise software into a unified object-centric operational field governed through receipted graph hooks, avatar-relative projections, and replayable process trajectories.

The central thesis is that traditional software architecture incorrectly treats persisted object state as the primary operational truth. We instead demonstrate that lawful operational motion—expressed as object-centric trajectories over graph conditions—forms the actual substrate of enterprise computation.

Building upon recent advances in Object-Centric Event Data (OCED), Object-Centric Process Querying (OCPQ), workflow-net decomposition, and Partially Ordered Workflow Language (POWL 2.0), we introduce a runtime architecture where:
- jobs-to-be-done (JTBDs) become executable hook tensions,
- workflows become admissible trajectory fields,
- permissions become avatar-relative graph projections,
- process history becomes a cryptographically chained receipt surface,
- and operational state becomes a compiled projection of lawful motion rather than a mutable source-of-truth database.

The resulting architecture replaces traditional frontend/backend separations with a multi-runtime autonomic membrane spanning:
- Expo client projections,
- Supabase Edge authoritative settlement,
- object-centric graph hooks,
- deterministic replay systems,
- and partial-order trajectory verification.

We further demonstrate that a single operational hook manifests differently across avatars while preserving shared operational truth. This yields a fundamentally new category of software system: avatar-relative operational computation.

## Chapter 1 — Introduction

### 1.1 The Failure of Classical Application Architecture
Conventional enterprise software evolved around three assumptions:
1. databases are the source of truth,
2. workflows are imperative scripts,
3. users interact through static role-based applications.

This architecture produced several pathological outcomes:

| Problem | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| CRUD primacy | loss of causal history |
| imperative workflows | brittle orchestration |
| page-centric UX | fragmentation of operational intent |
| backend/frontend split | duplicated logic |
| role-based rendering | static visibility surfaces |
| audit after execution | unverifiable operational causality |

Traditional workflow systems capture mutations but not operational lawfulness. The database answers “What exists now?” but not “Why did this become admissible?”

### 1.2 Object-Centric Operational Reality
Object-centric process research demonstrated that real enterprise processes do not revolve around a single case identifier. Instead, customers, orders, items, employees, schedules, notifications, volunteers, and assets all participate simultaneously in overlapping operational trajectories.

OCED and OCPQ demonstrated that operational querying becomes dramatically more expressive once object interactions become primary. This dissertation extends that realization: **The operational unit is not the workflow. The operational unit is the hook-triggered object trajectory.**

## Chapter 2 — The Hook Inversion

### 2.1 From Workflows to Hooks
Traditional workflow engines define ordered steps, transitions, states, and approval chains.
We instead define: `condition → tension → autonomic intervention → projection → receipt → replay`
This becomes the fundamental execution primitive. We call this primitive: **the VKG Hook.**

### 2.2 Hooks as Operational Physics
Hooks are not UI callbacks, webhooks, or frontend lifecycle methods. Hooks are **graph-reactive autonomic operational laws.**

Each hook evaluates object-centric graph conditions, authority constraints, trajectory admissibility, replay stability, and projection rules. This converts software from object mutation into operational field evolution.

### 2.3 Jobs-To-Be-Done as Hook Tensions
A major outcome of the research was the realization that JTBDs are not product requirements. JTBDs are recurring operational tensions.

**Example:**
*Traditional formulation:* “When volunteers are missing, staff need coverage.”

*VKG-HR formulation:*
- **Condition:** `volunteer_count < threshold`
- **Tension:** operational staffing instability
- **Autonomic:** shortage detection hook
- **Effects:** avatar-relative intervention projections
- **Settlement:** receipted assignment trajectory

Thus, JTBDs become executable graph hooks.

## Chapter 3 — Avatar-Relative Operational Projection

### 3.1 Shared Operational Reality
A single operational hook produces one causal event, one authoritative settlement, and one receipt chain. However, each avatar experiences the hook differently. This produces **avatar-relative operational projection.**

### 3.2 The Avatar Projection Calculus
A single hook manifests differently according to authority, visibility, operational role, trajectory relevance, and policy projection.

| Avatar | Projection |

Thus: Hooks remain globally true while projections remain locally admissible.

### 3.3 Operational Relativity
Classical applications create separate dashboards, APIs, and business logic. The VKG runtime instead computes a unified operational truth, then projects admissible slices. This transforms software into a projection field rather than a page hierarchy.
